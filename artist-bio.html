Pablo Picasso

Born 1881 | Died 1973

Pablo Picasso, born on October 25, 1881, in MÃ¡laga, Spain, was a highly influential artist known for his versatile and pioneering contributions to art in the 20th century.

He co-founded the Cubist movement, was instrumental in the invention of constructed sculpture, and made significant contributions to Symbolism and Surrealism.

His prolific output included over 20,000 paintings, prints, drawings, sculptures, ceramics, theater sets, and costumes.

Picasso's revolutionary artistic accomplishments brought him universal renown and immense fortune throughout his life, making him one of the best-known figures in 20th-century art.
